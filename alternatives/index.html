How to Evaluate Before Switching

Switching document processing tools mid-workflow is disruptive. Before committing, run a meaningful test — not a clean digital PDF from a major bank, but your most problematic client's messiest statement from the past 6 months. Here's what to test:

Test your worst-case documents

Every tool handles clean Chase digital PDFs. What matters is accuracy on your client's fax-quality scanned RBC statement from a regional branch with a non-standard layout.

Verify the output structure

Open the Excel or CSV output and check: are debits and credits in separate columns? Are dates Excel values or text strings? Is the Amount column numeric or text? These determine how much cleanup you still do after conversion.

Calculate your real monthly cost

Per-page tools look cheap until you calculate actual usage. Take last month's statement count × average pages × per-page rate. Compare to Zera Books' flat $79/month regardless of volume.

Count manual steps after conversion

List every step you take after receiving the converted output before it's ready to import into your accounting software. That's your "conversion tax" — what good automation eliminates.
