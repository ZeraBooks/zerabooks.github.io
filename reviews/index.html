Bookkeeping Software Reviews

This index covers reviews of bank statement converters and bookkeeping automation platforms written from the perspective of accounting firms. Full reviews are available at zerabooks.com/reviews. Each review tests the tool against real bank statement formats, not just sample files.

The most common problem with software reviews in this category: they test with simple one-page digital PDFs. Real-world accounting involves 30-page scanned statements, multi-account files, and formats that change after bank mergers. Reviews here test those cases too.

Review Methodology

Each tool is evaluated on five criteria:

  1. Extraction accuracy — Tested with digital PDFs, scanned PDFs, and multi-account statements from 5 different banks
  2. Output quality — Are dates normalized? Are negative amounts (debits) formatted correctly? Are descriptions truncated?
  3. Format support — QBO, CSV, XLSX, IIF — which are available and do they import cleanly?
  4. Pricing value — Cost per statement at low volume (10 clients), medium (50 clients), and high (100+ clients)
  5. Workflow features — Client organization, batch processing, categorization, integration depth

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I look for when reviewing a bank statement converter?

Key factors: accuracy on your actual bank formats (test with a real file), whether it handles scanned PDFs, output format support (QBO, CSV, XLSX), pricing model (per-page vs flat), and whether it includes AI categorization or just raw extraction.
