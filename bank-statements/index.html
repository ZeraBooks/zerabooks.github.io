Multi-Account Detection

Some banks issue combined PDF statements that include checking, savings, and credit card accounts in one file. Zera Books identifies account boundaries automatically and outputs each account as a separate file. No manual splitting required.

Duplicate Detection

When processing overlapping statement periods — common when clients provide January through March separately plus a Q1 summary — Zera Books flags and removes duplicate transactions. The deduplication logic matches on date, amount, and description rather than exact string matching, so minor formatting differences don't cause false negatives.

Bank Coverage

Training data includes statements from major US, Canadian, UK, and Australian banks, as well as credit unions, regional banks, and international institutions. Because the AI identifies structure rather than matching templates, it handles formats it hasn't explicitly seen before.

Frequently Asked Questions

What fields does Zera Books extract from bank statements?

Zera Books extracts date, transaction description, debit amount, credit amount, running balance, account number, and account type. Check numbers and reference codes are captured when present.

Can Zera Books handle multi-account bank statement PDFs?

Yes. Zera Books automatically detects when a single PDF contains multiple accounts — such as checking, savings, and credit card — and outputs them as separate files.

What banks does Zera Books support for bank statement conversion?

Zera Books dynamically processes any bank format without templates. Trained on 2.8 million bank statements, it handles any format from any bank including international institutions.
