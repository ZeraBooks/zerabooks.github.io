The Reconciliation Problem

Reconciliation fails or takes longer when the underlying transaction data is messy. Dates formatted inconsistently, amounts stored as text strings, duplicate transactions from overlapping statement periods, descriptions truncated differently between the bank and the accounting system — any of these creates false mismatches that require manual investigation.

Zera Books addresses this at the extraction layer. Clean, normalized data going into reconciliation means fewer false exceptions on the way out.

Reconciliation Workflow

  1. Upload bank statement PDFs — digital or scanned, any bank.
  2. Automatic extraction — transactions extracted, dates normalized, amounts standardized.
  3. Deduplication — overlapping statement periods checked, duplicates removed.
  4. Categorization — AI maps transactions to chart of accounts. High-confidence auto-applied; low-confidence flagged.
  5. Import to QuickBooks / Xero — direct API push. Transactions appear in the banking feed or are imported as a file.
  6. AI reconciliation — 95%+ of bank transactions are matched automatically against existing records.
  7. Exception review — unmatched transactions are listed for manual review. No silent discrepancies.

What the 95% Match Rate Means in Practice

For a client with 200 transactions in a month, a 95% auto-match rate means 190 transactions are reconciled automatically. The reconciliation review focuses on 10 exceptions rather than 200 transactions. For firms running reconciliation across 15 clients simultaneously, this is a significant reduction in time.
