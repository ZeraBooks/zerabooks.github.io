Zera OCR — Scanned Document Processing

Zera OCR handles the portion of documents that don't have a selectable text layer — scanned bank statements, photographed invoices, faxed financial records, and image-only PDFs. It's integrated directly into the Zera Books processing pipeline, so scanned documents go through the same extraction and categorization workflow as digital ones.

95%+ accuracy on standard-quality scans. Performance depends on scan resolution and document condition. Optimal at 300 DPI.

  • Faxed documents converted to PDF

  • Check scans (with dedicated MICR layer for MICR font recognition)

Mixed Document Handling

A common real-world scenario: a client sends a PDF that's partly digital (the current year's statements generated by the bank's PDF system) and partly scanned (older statements they had to photocopy). Zera Books detects which pages have a text layer on a page-by-page basis. Digital pages go through direct text extraction; scanned pages go through OCR. The output is a single combined file with all transactions regardless of which pages were which type.

Post-OCR Processing

After OCR runs, the same Zera AI extraction and data cleaning steps apply. Dates get normalized, amounts get standardized, duplicates get flagged. The OCR output feeds directly into the same pipeline as digital documents — you don't handle scanned and digital documents differently.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Zera Books convert scanned bank statements to Excel?

Yes. Zera OCR converts scanned bank statement PDFs and image files to structured Excel or CSV. It achieves 95%+ accuracy on standard scan quality and handles lower-resolution scans with reduced accuracy.

What scan quality does Zera OCR require?

Zera OCR performs best at 300 DPI or higher. It handles lower-resolution scans but accuracy decreases below 150 DPI. Both black-and-white and color scans are supported.

Can Zera Books handle a PDF that has both digital and scanned pages?

Yes. Zera Books automatically detects which pages have a text layer and which are image-only, applying text extraction to digital pages and OCR to scanned pages within the same document.
