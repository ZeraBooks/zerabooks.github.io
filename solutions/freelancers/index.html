Freelancer Bookkeeping: Automating Statement Processing and Expense Tracking

Freelancers spend hours on bookkeeping that adds no value to their actual work. Downloading 12 months of bank statements, extracting transactions into a spreadsheet, sorting income from expenses, and categorizing everything before handing it to an accountant for tax time — it's tedious, repetitive, and prone to errors when you're doing it manually.

What Freelancers Actually Need

Most freelancers don't need a full accounting platform. They need transactions in a usable format, categorized well enough to prepare a Schedule C or hand to their accountant. Zera Books converts bank statement PDFs into categorized Excel or CSV files in minutes, without requiring any accounting software.

Typical freelancer workflow: Download monthly statement PDF from your bank → upload to Zera Books → download categorized Excel → share with accountant at year-end. No subscription to accounting software required.

Income and Expense Categorization

The AI categorization assigns transactions to income and expense categories based on description patterns. Client payments get categorized as income; software subscriptions, travel, and home office expenses get their respective categories. For recurring vendors — your cloud storage, your project management tool, your coworking space — the model learns after the first review and assigns consistently going forward.

Confidence scores are shown for each categorization. Transactions the model isn't certain about are flagged, so you're not blindly approving outputs for tax purposes.

Invoice Extraction for Client Billing Records

Freelancers often need to match their bank deposits against invoices issued to clients. Zera Books extracts invoice data — client name, invoice number, amount, line items, tax, and date — from PDF invoices. You can then cross-reference deposit entries in your bank statement export against the invoice export to confirm which invoices have been paid.

Quarterly Tax Prep in Under 30 Minutes

  1. Upload the last three months of bank statements in a single batch
  2. Review AI-categorized transactions, adjust any misclassifications
  3. Export to Excel with income and expense totals by category
  4. Use totals for estimated tax calculations or send directly to your accountant

For freelancers who bill in multiple currencies, amounts are extracted with the original currency code — no manual conversion guessing required.
