Bulk Invoice OCR: Extracting Data from Batches of Invoice PDFs

Accounts payable processing is one of the highest-volume document tasks in any business. A company receiving 200 vendor invoices a month spends significant time on manual entry — keying the same fields from each PDF into accounting software. Bulk OCR extraction eliminates that step.

What Gets Extracted from Invoice PDFs

Zera Books extracts structured data from each invoice in a batch:

  • Vendor name and address
  • Invoice number and date
  • Due date and payment terms
  • Line items: description, quantity, unit price, line total
  • Subtotal, tax amount (with tax code if present), and total
  • PO number if referenced

All fields are output in structured rows — one row per line item in Excel or CSV, or a summary row per invoice depending on your workflow preference.

No Template Training Required

Trained on 420,000+ invoices: Zera Books' AI was trained on 420,000 invoice documents. It reads new vendor layouts without any configuration — no mapping fields, no drawing zones, no training runs per vendor.

Template-based invoice OCR tools require you to define where each field appears on the page for every vendor. When a vendor updates their invoice layout, the template breaks. Zera Books adapts dynamically because the model understands invoice structure rather than relying on pixel positions.

Batch Processing for Accounts Payable

Upload a full month of vendor invoices in one batch. Zera Books queues and processes them concurrently, handling both digital PDFs and scanned paper invoices in the same batch. The OCR engine achieves 95%+ accuracy on scanned documents, including invoices received via fax or photographed with a phone.
