Getting Clean Financial Data Out of Client Documents for Tax Prep

Tax season document processing is a volume problem. A mid-size accounting firm can receive hundreds of bank statements, year-end financial reports, and invoice packages in January and February. Most of that arrives as PDFs — some digital, some scanned. Getting usable data out of those files manually is slow and error-prone.

Document Types Relevant to Tax Engagements

Zera Books processes all four document types a tax preparer typically receives from a client:

  • Bank statements — transaction-level extraction with date, description, amount, and running balance
  • Financial statements — P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow with multi-period parsing
  • Invoices — line items, tax amounts, vendor name, and totals for deduction verification
  • Checks — MICR data extraction for payment reconciliation

Most document extraction tools only handle bank statements. When a client sends a P&L PDF alongside their statements, you either re-key it manually or export it to a separate tool. Zera Books processes all of these in the same workflow.

OCR for Scanned Documents

Scanned statement handling: Zera Books' OCR achieves 95%+ accuracy on scanned PDFs, including low-quality photocopies and rotated pages. This covers the older statements clients dig out for multi-year reviews.

Password-protected PDFs are also supported. The client provides the password at upload, and the file is processed normally. Extracted data is deleted from Zera Books' servers after 30 days — useful for clients concerned about document retention.

Categorization for Tax Classification

AI transaction categorization maps line items to standard charts of accounts. For tax preparation, this means income and expense categories are pre-populated, giving you a starting point rather than a blank ledger. The categorization uses confidence scores — transactions the model is uncertain about are flagged for review, so you're not approving outputs you can't verify.
