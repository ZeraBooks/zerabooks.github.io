Bank Statement Processing for Small Business Bookkeeping

Most small business owners don't have a dedicated bookkeeper. They have a spreadsheet, a bank account, and a stack of PDF statements they need to reconcile at month-end. Getting transactions out of those PDFs without typing each one by hand is where Zera Books fits in.

The PDF-to-Spreadsheet Problem

Banks provide statements as PDFs because the format is consistent for display. It's not designed for data extraction. Copy-pasting from a PDF into Excel loses formatting, scrambles numbers, and misses rows. Adobe Acrobat's export function is unreliable on bank statements because of how the tables are encoded.

Zera Books extracts the underlying transaction data — date, description, debit, credit, and balance — and outputs it in clean rows. The AI handles format variations between banks without requiring any setup specific to your institution.

Multiple Accounts in One Upload

Multi-account detection: If you run a business checking account, a savings account, and a business credit card, you can upload all three statements at once. Zera Books separates them automatically — no manual sorting.

This matters for small business owners who receive a combined statement PDF from their bank. Zera Books identifies the account boundaries and exports each one separately, with the correct opening and closing balances.

Data Cleaning Included

Raw bank statement data often has inconsistent date formats (01/15/25 vs January 15, 2025), truncated descriptions, and encoding artifacts from PDF extraction. Zera Books standardizes dates, normalizes description text, and flags any values that look anomalous before output.

For multi-currency accounts, amounts are extracted with the original currency code intact so you can handle conversion in your accounting software rather than losing that information at extraction time.
