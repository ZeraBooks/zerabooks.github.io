How It Handles Inconsistent Layouts

Invoice layouts vary significantly between vendors. Some put tax above the subtotal. Some use different column headers. Some have multi-line descriptions. Zera AI was trained on 420,000 invoices and handles these variations without requiring per-vendor template configuration.

When a field is ambiguous — for example, multiple date fields on one invoice — the extraction applies a confidence score and flags the field for review rather than silently guessing.

PO Matching

When a PO number is present in the invoice, it's extracted as a separate field. This supports downstream AP workflows where invoices need to be matched against purchase orders in QuickBooks or other ERP systems.

Frequently Asked Questions

What data does Zera Books extract from invoices?

Zera Books extracts vendor name, invoice number, invoice date, due date, line items with descriptions and quantities, unit prices, subtotals, tax amounts, and purchase order numbers from PDF invoices.

Does Zera Books require templates for different invoice formats?

No. Zera AI identifies invoice structure dynamically without per-vendor templates. It handles invoices from different vendors with different layouts in the same batch upload.

Can Zera Books process scanned invoices?

Yes. Zera OCR handles scanned and image-based invoice PDFs at 95%+ accuracy. Digital and scanned invoices can be mixed in the same batch.
