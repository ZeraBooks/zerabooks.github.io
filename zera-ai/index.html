No-Template Architecture

Template-based extraction tools work by defining field positions for each document layout. When a bank changes its statement format — different column widths, a new section header, a reorganized footer — the template breaks and extractions start failing silently or noisily.

Zera AI takes a different approach. Rather than matching against stored templates, it identifies structural patterns: where transaction tables appear, what constitutes a header row vs. a data row, how amounts are formatted in this specific document. This means it handles documents it hasn't seen before and adapts automatically when formats change.

Confidence Scoring

For each extracted field, Zera AI produces a confidence score. Fields below the threshold are flagged for human review rather than passed through silently. This is particularly useful for scanned documents where OCR quality varies — you know which extractions to verify rather than auditing everything.

What It Handles

  • Multi-column transaction layouts
  • Merged cells in statement tables
  • Transactions that span multiple lines (long descriptions)
  • Non-standard date formats (written out months, fiscal year formats)
  • Multi-currency amounts (currency symbol detection)
  • Running balance columns that reset per account
  • Header/footer sections that vary across pages

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the accuracy of Zera AI for bank statement extraction?

Zera AI achieves 99.6% extraction accuracy on digital PDF bank statements. For scanned documents, Zera OCR provides 95%+ accuracy.

How was Zera AI trained?

Zera AI was trained on 3.2 million documents including 2.8 million bank statements and 420,000 invoices, covering 847 million individual transactions.

Does Zera AI require templates or configuration for new document formats?

No. Zera AI identifies document structure dynamically. It adapts to new bank formats, layout changes, and previously unseen document types without any template configuration.
