Truist Bank Statement Converter (Formerly SunTrust and BB&T)

Truist Financial was formed in 2019 through the merger of BB&T and SunTrust Banks, with full client migration completed by 2022. Accountants working with Truist clients may have three different statement formats: old BB&T PDFs, old SunTrust PDFs, and current Truist PDFs — all from the same client.

Truist PDF Statement Format

Current Truist statements use a clean table layout: Date, Description, Debit (-), Credit (+), Balance. Business checking statements add a section for outstanding checks and service charges below the main transaction table.

SunTrust-era statements used a slightly different column order with separate "Withdrawals" and "Deposits" columns. BB&T statements listed check transactions separately from electronic transactions in a grouped layout similar to Fifth Third.

Downloading Truist Statements

In Truist Online Banking: go to your account > Statements to download PDFs. For transaction exports, go to Transaction History and use the download icon to select CSV, OFX, or QFX. Historical statements from before the Truist migration (BB&T or SunTrust era) may need to be requested from a branch if not available online.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I download Truist bank statements online?

Log into Truist Online Banking, select your account, then click Statements to download PDFs. Transaction history exports (CSV, OFX, QFX) are available from the Transaction History page. Truist provides up to 7 years of PDF statements.

I have old SunTrust statements — can those be converted?

Yes. SunTrust became Truist in 2022. Old SunTrust-branded PDFs use a different layout than current Truist PDFs but convert accurately. Upload SunTrust PDFs to Zera Books the same way as Truist statements.

Does Truist support QuickBooks bank feed?

Yes. Truist supports direct QuickBooks Online bank feed integration. For historical statements, download PDFs and convert to QBO using Zera Books. Old BB&T accounts migrated to Truist may need PDF conversion for the transition period.
