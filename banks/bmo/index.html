BMO PDF Statement Format

BMO personal checking statements use a standard table: Date, Description, Withdrawals, Deposits, Balance. Transaction descriptions include the payee name for Interac debit purchases, cheque number for cheques, and the sending/receiving institution for wire transfers.

BMO Business Banking statements are often more detailed — they include a period summary at the top (number of deposits, number of withdrawals, service charges), followed by the transaction list. The service charge line items at the bottom of business statements are actual charges and should be included in the transaction import, not excluded.

BMO Harris Bank (US)

BMO Harris Bank statements (US clients) use a similar format to Canadian BMO but with USD amounts and MM/DD/YYYY date format. Business accounts with BMO Harris often have a "Previous Balance" header row at the top of the transaction table that must be excluded from conversion. Zera Books handles this automatically for both BMO Canada and BMO Harris US formats.

BMO CSV Format Details

BMO's CSV export uses a signed Transaction Amount column (negative for withdrawals) which is actually closer to what QuickBooks and Xero expect than most Canadian bank exports. You still need to remove the Balance column and adjust the date format (DD/MM/YYYY to MM/DD/YYYY for US-region QuickBooks). Zera Books outputs clean formatted CSV from the source PDF.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I download BMO Bank of Montreal statements as PDF?

Log into BMO Online Banking, go to Accounts, select your account, then click Statements. eStatements are downloadable as PDF for up to 7 years. BMO also offers statement downloads from BMO Business Banking Online for business accounts.

Does BMO support QuickBooks Online Canada bank feeds?

Yes. BMO connects to QuickBooks Online Canada for direct bank feeds. For older statements or the BMO Harris US accounts, download PDF statements and convert to QBO using Zera Books.

What is the BMO CSV export format?

BMO CSV exports use: Date, Payee, Transaction Amount, Balance. The date is in DD/MM/YYYY format. A negative Transaction Amount is a debit (withdrawal). This format works after removing the Balance column and converting the date to your accounting software's expected format.
