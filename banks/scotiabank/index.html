Scotiabank PDF Statement Format

Scotiabank personal account PDFs list transactions with Date, Description, Withdrawals, Deposits, Balance. Business account statements (especially Scotia Business Banking) include additional sections: outstanding cheques, pre-authorized debits, and service charge summaries. These sections appear at the end of the statement.

Scotiabank commercial account statements (for larger businesses) are often multi-page with account summaries on the first page followed by detailed transaction pages. Zera Books processes all pages and excludes summary section figures from the transaction list.

Scotiabank CSV Export Quirk

Scotiabank's CSV transaction export uses separate Debit and Credit columns rather than a signed Amount column. The date format is typically YYYY/MM/DD. Both of these need to be adjusted before importing into QuickBooks (which expects signed Amount in MM/DD/YYYY). Zera Books handles this transformation during conversion.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I download Scotiabank statements as PDF?

Log into Scotiabank Online Banking (Scotia OnLine), go to My Accounts, select the account, then click View Statements. eStatements are available as PDF for up to 7 years. Download each month as a separate PDF.

Does Scotiabank support QuickBooks Online Canada bank feeds?

Yes. Scotiabank connects directly to QuickBooks Online Canada for bank feeds. For historical statements, download PDFs and convert to QBO using Zera Books. Scotiabank also offers OFX downloads from the transaction export screen.

What does Scotiabank's CSV export look like?

Scotiabank CSV exports include: Date, Transaction, Debit, Credit, Balance. The date format is DD/MM/YYYY or YYYY/MM/DD depending on the account type. Separate Debit and Credit columns need to be merged into a single signed Amount for most accounting software imports.
