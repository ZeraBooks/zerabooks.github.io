About PDF Format Differences

Digital PDFs (those generated directly from banking software) contain an embedded text layer and convert quickly and accurately. Scanned PDFs, common for older statements or paper originals, require OCR — Zera Books achieves 95%+ accuracy on scanned documents.

Two-column statement layouts, combined savings/checking pages, and running-balance columns (common in Canadian banks) are handled automatically without template configuration.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert a Chase bank statement PDF to Excel?

Upload the Chase PDF to Zera Books. Chase statements use a consistent column layout that handles accurately, including checking, savings, and credit card statements.

Can I convert a TD Bank statement PDF to QuickBooks?

Yes. TD Bank statements (US and Canadian) can be converted to QBO format, which imports directly into QuickBooks Online's bank feed without column mapping.

Why do some banks' PDFs fail to convert correctly?

Scanned PDFs require OCR. Some banks use two-column layouts or nested tables that rule-based converters misread — AI-based tools handle these cases more reliably because they understand layout context, not just text position.
