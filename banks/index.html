Why "Any Bank Format" Is Different From a Bank List

Template-based converters maintain a list of supported banks. When your bank updates its statement layout — new header, different date format, added account section — the converter breaks until the template is manually updated. The "supported bank" list is actually a list of banks whose templates someone has built and maintained.

Zera Books takes a different approach. Zera AI was trained on 2.8 million bank statements from hundreds of institutions. It identifies fields by what they mean — the date of the transaction, the description, the withdrawal amount — not by where they appear on the page. A layout change doesn't require any template update.

What this means for you: When your client's regional credit union updates its statement format in February, or when an international client sends a statement from a bank Zera Books has never seen, processing still works without any configuration. See all 45 bank guides on Zera Books.
