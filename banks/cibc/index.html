CIBC PDF Statement Format

CIBC personal account statements use a clean layout: Date, Description, Withdrawals, Deposits, Balance. The description is typically the payee name or transaction code plus location. CIBC uses specific prefix codes for transaction types: "POS" for debit card, "PAP" for Pre-Authorized Payment, "ABM" for ATM.

CIBC Business Account statements include a summary section at the top with opening balance, total deposits, total withdrawals, and closing balance before the transaction detail. These summary figures must be excluded from the import. Zera Books identifies and skips the CIBC summary block.

CIBC US Dollar Accounts

CIBC US dollar personal and business accounts produce statements in USD. The PDF format is identical to CAD statements. When converting, Zera Books outputs amounts in the currency shown on the statement — ensure your accounting software account is set to USD before importing a USD CIBC statement.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I download CIBC statements as PDF?

Log into CIBC Online Banking, go to Accounts, select your account, then click eStatements. CIBC eStatements are available as PDF for the most recent 7 years. Each statement is a separate PDF download by month.

Does CIBC support QuickBooks Online Canada bank feeds?

Yes. CIBC connects directly to QuickBooks Online Canada. For historical statements not covered by the feed, download PDF eStatements and convert to QBO using Zera Books.

What format does CIBC use for transaction exports?

CIBC exports transactions in CSV format from the Account Details page. The CSV uses columns: Date, Description, Debit, Credit, Balance. Date format is DD/MM/YYYY. The Debit and Credit are separate positive columns — combine them into a signed Amount for QuickBooks or Xero import.
