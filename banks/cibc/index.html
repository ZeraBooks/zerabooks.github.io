CIBC Statement Formats and What Gets Extracted

CIBC issues several distinct statement formats depending on account type. Zera Books processes all of them without configuration — from personal chequing and savings to business accounts, credit cards, and Simplii Financial (CIBC's direct banking brand).

Personal chequing and savings statements from CIBC Bank use a standard three-column layout: date, transaction description, and running balance. Credits and debits are in a single amount column with the sign indicating direction. Zera Books correctly maps these to separate debit/credit columns in the output.

Business banking statements — including the CIBC Business Operating Account and CIBC Smart Business Account — often include a dedicated "Cheques Paid" section at the end, listing cleared checks by check number. Zera Books parses this section and integrates the entries into the chronological transaction list.

Personal Accounts

Everyday Chequing, Smart, Unlimited, Dividend accounts — all standard layouts extracted cleanly.

Credit Cards

Aventura Visa, Dividend Visa, Costco Mastercard — reward columns isolated, only financial data extracted.

Business Accounts

Business Operating Account, Smart Business — check register sections merged into chronological output.

Simplii Financial

Simplii chequing, savings, and VISA — CIBC-infrastructure statements recognized natively.

CIBC Aventura Visa and Dividend Visa statements include reward point columns alongside transaction amounts. These columns display points earned per transaction and cumulative balance — not dollar values. Generic converters that treat all numeric columns as financial data will import these point values as transaction amounts, resulting in severely inflated figures. Zera Books identifies and excludes reward columns automatically.

CAD/USD Multi-Currency and Multi-Account Detection

CIBC is one of Canada's Big Five banks with a significant international client base, particularly in trade finance and cross-border banking. Many CIBC business clients hold both CAD and USD accounts — sometimes receiving a combined PDF from CIBC that contains both account statements.

Zera Books handles CIBC's multi-currency statements in two ways:

  • Single-currency accounts: Currency detected from the statement header (CAD or USD), currency code added as a column in every row of the output file.
  • Combined statements: When CIBC bundles multiple accounts into one PDF, Zera Books' multi-account detection splits the file — one output file per account, each with its correct currency code.

This is especially important for bookkeepers handling CIBC clients in the import/export sector, where USD invoices are paid from USD chequing and the CAD operating account is reconciled separately. Zera Books' Zera OCR also handles scanned CIBC statements where currency symbols may not be machine-readable.

Frequently Asked Questions — CIBC Statement Conversion

Can Zera Books convert CIBC PDF statements that include both CAD and USD accounts?

Yes. Zera Books detects the currency per account and preserves the currency code in a dedicated column in your output file. CAD and USD CIBC accounts are kept separate with proper currency labeling — no manual reformatting needed.

Does Zera Books support Simplii Financial statements?

Yes. Simplii Financial uses the same back-end infrastructure as CIBC, and Zera Books recognizes Simplii statement headers and transaction formats natively. No configuration is needed — upload a Simplii PDF the same way as a CIBC PDF.

How does Zera Books handle CIBC credit card statements with rewards points columns?

CIBC Aventura and Dividend Visa statements include reward point columns beside transaction amounts. Zera Books isolates the monetary columns (amount, balance) and excludes point columns from the financial output, preventing incorrect values in your bookkeeping file.
