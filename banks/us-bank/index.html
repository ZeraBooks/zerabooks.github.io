US Bank CSV Format

US Bank CSV downloads typically start with a few rows of account metadata before the transaction data begins. The transaction columns are:

  • Date (MM/DD/YYYY)
  • Transaction (description)
  • Name (payee or additional description)
  • Memo (reference text)
  • Amount (signed — negative for debits)

The "Name" column often contains useful payee information that the "Transaction" column doesn't. When converting, it helps to merge these two columns for better transaction descriptions in your accounting software.

US Bank Business Accounts

US Bank business checking and business savings statements use a slightly different format than personal accounts. Business PDFs often include a "Previous Balance" and "New Balance" line at the end of the transaction list. These must be excluded from the imported transactions.

US Bank business accounts with multiple signatories sometimes receive statements that don't have a direct online download option — paper statements can be scanned and processed with Zera Books OCR.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I download my US Bank statement as a CSV?

In US Bank online banking, go to Accounts > Account Activity > Download. You can select a date range and choose CSV, OFX, or QFX format. PDF statements are available under Statements & Notices.

Does US Bank offer QBO files for QuickBooks?

Yes. US Bank online banking offers QFX (Quicken) and OFX downloads from the Account Activity download menu. QFX files import directly into QuickBooks Online. For the best results, use the PDF and convert via Zera Books for full control over the output format.

Why does my US Bank CSV export have extra rows?

US Bank CSV exports include a header row with account information and sometimes a balance row at the top and bottom. These must be removed before importing into QuickBooks or Xero. Zera Books removes these automatically during conversion.
