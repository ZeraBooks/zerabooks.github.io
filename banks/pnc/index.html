PNC PDF Statement Quirks

PNC business account PDFs sometimes include a "Virtual Wallet" summary section at the top before transaction detail. Converters that don't handle this will include the summary figures as transactions. Zera Books recognizes the PNC statement structure and ignores non-transaction sections.

PNC statements for accounts with multiple account numbers on the same statement (e.g., checking and money market combined) are handled by Zera Books' multi-account detection — each account gets a separate output file.

PNC CSV Export Limitations

PNC's CSV download uses this column structure:

ColumnFormat
DateMM/DD/YYYY
DescriptionText
WithdrawalsPositive (money out)
DepositsPositive (money in)
BalanceRunning balance

This does not match what QuickBooks or Xero expect for CSV import. You need to combine Withdrawals and Deposits into a single signed Amount column and remove the Balance column before importing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What formats can I download PNC Bank statements in?

PNC Online Banking allows download of statements as PDF or CSV. PNC also supports OFX and QFX downloads for direct bank feed connections to QuickBooks. PDF statements are available for up to 7 years online.

Does PNC Bank CSV export work with QuickBooks directly?

PNC's CSV export uses a 6-column format: Date, Description, Withdrawals (-), Deposits (+), Balance. QuickBooks requires different columns, so the PNC CSV needs reformatting before import. Use Zera Books to handle this automatically.

How do I convert a PNC PDF statement to Excel?

Upload the PNC PDF to Zera Books and select Excel as the output format. PNC statements are digital PDFs (not scanned), so extraction is accurate and fast. Multi-page PNC statements are supported.
