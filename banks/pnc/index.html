PNC Bank Statement Formats and What Gets Extracted

PNC generates several distinct statement layouts depending on account type. Personal Virtual Wallet statements divide transactions across Spend, Reserve, and Growth sub-accounts on a single PDF — a layout that trips up most generic converters. Business checking statements use a different column order, and older paper statements from PNC's mid-Atlantic and Midwest branches use a two-column transaction layout that requires proper OCR handling.

How to Convert PNC Bank Statements: Step by Step

The process takes three steps. There is no account linking, no credential sharing with PNC, and no template setup. You upload the PDF, Zera AI processes it, and you download a clean file ready for QuickBooks, Xero, or Excel.

Download your PNC statement PDF

Log into PNC Online Banking, navigate to Statements, and download the monthly PDF. For business accounts, select the specific account and period. Password-protected PDFs are supported — enter the password at upload.

PNC allows up to 7 years of statement history in Online Banking. For year-end reconciliation, download all 12 months and batch-upload them to Zera Books at once.

Upload to Zera Books

Drag the PDF into Zera Books or select it from your client folder. For multiple months, select all files at once — batch processing handles up to 50 statements simultaneously.

Download your formatted output

Choose Excel, CSV, QBO (QuickBooks Online), IIF (QuickBooks Desktop), or pre-formatted Xero CSV. Transactions are already AI-categorized — review suggestions and adjust any before export.

The QBO file imports directly into QuickBooks Online as a bank feed. The Xero CSV maps to Zera Books' Xero import format with account codes pre-populated.
