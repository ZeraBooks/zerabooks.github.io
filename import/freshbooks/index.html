FreshBooks Bank Statement Import: Step-by-Step Guide

FreshBooks supports two ways to get bank transactions in: a live bank connection via Plaid, or a manual CSV import. The manual import is essential for historical data, unsupported banks, or clients who don't want to give FreshBooks bank access.

Manual CSV Import Steps

  1. In FreshBooks, go to Accounting > Bank Accounts
  2. Select the account or click Add Account if it doesn't exist yet
  3. Click Import Transactions
  4. Upload your CSV file
  5. Map the columns: Date, Description, Amount
  6. FreshBooks will preview the transactions before confirming

CSV Column Requirements

ColumnFormatNotes
DateMM/DD/YYYYRequired
DescriptionTextRequired — appears as transaction name
AmountNegative for withdrawalsRequired, or use Debit/Credit columns
DebitPositive numberOptional alternative
CreditPositive numberOptional alternative

FreshBooks is case-insensitive for column headers, so "date", "DATE", and "Date" all work. Don't include blank rows at the top or bottom — FreshBooks will attempt to parse them as transactions and fail.

Bank Connection vs Manual Import

FreshBooks connects to most US and Canadian banks via Plaid. If your client's bank is supported, the live connection is easier for ongoing bookkeeping. Use manual CSV import for:

  • Banks not supported by Plaid (many credit unions, international banks)
  • Historical statements older than what the feed provides (usually 90 days)
  • Audits requiring exact statement data rather than feed data

After Import: Categorizing Transactions

FreshBooks assigns a default category to imported transactions. You'll need to review and recode them. FreshBooks will remember your category choices for recurring payees. Frequently Asked Questions

Can FreshBooks import bank statements from a CSV file?

Yes. FreshBooks accepts CSV imports under Accounting > Bank Accounts > Import Transactions. The required columns are Date, Description, and Amount (or separate Debit/Credit columns).

How do I get PDF bank statements into FreshBooks?

FreshBooks cannot read PDF files. Use Zera Books to convert the PDF to CSV, then import the CSV through FreshBooks' bank import feature. The conversion takes under a minute for most statements.

Does FreshBooks support OFX or QBO imports?

No. FreshBooks only supports CSV for manual transaction imports. OFX and QBO formats are not accepted. For direct feeds, FreshBooks connects via Plaid to supported banks.
