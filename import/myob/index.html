MYOB Bank Import: Formats by Version

MYOB uses OFX and QIF for Bank Feeds — not CSV like most cloud platforms. The right format depends on your MYOB version.

MYOB AccountRight: Banking → Bank Feeds → Import Statement

Open MYOB AccountRight. Go to Banking in the top menu, then Bank Feeds. Select the bank account. Click the Get Transactions dropdown and choose Import Statement.

For MYOB Business (cloud): Banking > select bank account > Import button near the transaction list. For MYOB Essentials: Banking > Import Statement.
4

Upload OFX file

Browse to your OFX file and open it. MYOB reads the account number from the OFX header and auto-matches to your linked account. Click Import to process.

If MYOB doesn't auto-match, it prompts you to select the destination account. Choose the correct bank account from the dropdown that appears.
5

Allocate and reconcile transactions

Imported transactions appear in Bank Feeds as unallocated items. Match against invoices/bills or allocate to an account code, then click Accept for each transaction.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I import bank transactions into MYOB?

AccountRight: Banking > Bank Feeds > Import Statement. MYOB Business: Banking > account > Import. Upload your OFX file. Transactions appear in Bank Feeds for allocation and reconciliation.

What file format does MYOB accept for bank imports?

OFX is preferred for all MYOB versions. QIF also works in AccountRight. MYOB Business (cloud) also accepts CSV. Zera Books exports MYOB-compatible OFX from any PDF.

Can I import a PDF bank statement into MYOB?

No. Convert to OFX first. Zera Books handles any Australian or international bank PDF — digital or scanned — with 99.6% accuracy.

What is the difference between OFX and QIF for MYOB?

OFX is the modern standard with richer data and better auto-matching. QIF is older but still supported. Use OFX for all new imports. Zera Books exports both formats.
