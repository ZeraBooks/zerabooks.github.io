CSV to Xero: Bank Statement Import Format Guide

Xero's CSV import is flexible about column names since you map them during upload, but the data format must be correct. This guide covers what Xero actually needs versus what trips people up.

Minimum Required CSV Format

A valid Xero CSV import needs at least two columns: Date and Amount. Everything else is optional but recommended for accurate reconciliation:

Amount Format Options

Xero accepts two amount conventions. Choose one and use it consistently throughout the file:

Option A — Signed single Amount column: Negative for withdrawals, positive for deposits.

01/15/2025,AMAZON MKTP,-52.99

Option B — Separate Spent and Received columns: Both always positive. Leave the other blank.

01/15/2025,AMAZON MKTP,52.99, (withdrawal)
01/16/2025,PAYROLL,,3200.00 (deposit)

Date Format Notes

Xero reads dates based on your organisation's regional setting. Check yours under Settings > General Settings > Organisation. Common settings:

  • US organisations: MM/DD/YYYY
  • UK/AU/NZ organisations: DD/MM/YYYY
  • ISO format: YYYY-MM-DD works in most Xero regions

If your file uses a different format than your Xero setting, dates from days 1-12 will silently swap month and day.

Import Steps

  1. Go to Accounting > Bank Accounts
  2. Click the account > Import a Statement
  3. Upload your CSV file
  4. On the column mapping screen, assign each column to the right field
  5. Set the date format to match your file
  6. Click Import and review in the Reconcile tab

Frequently Asked Questions

What columns does Xero require for CSV bank import?

Xero requires at minimum: Date and Amount. Optional but recommended: Payee, Description, Reference. You map the columns during upload so header names don't need to match exactly.

What date format does Xero CSV import accept?

Xero accepts the date format set in your organisation settings. For US organisations: MM/DD/YYYY. For UK/AU/NZ: DD/MM/YYYY. The format in your CSV must match your Xero setting.

Does Xero import negative amounts for debits in CSV?

Yes. Xero uses negative amounts for money leaving the account (debits/withdrawals) and positive for money coming in (credits/deposits). You can also use separate Spent and Received columns with positive values only.
