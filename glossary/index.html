Accounting & Bookkeeping Automation Glossary

Definitions for terms used in bank statement processing, PDF conversion, and bookkeeping automation. The full glossary is at zerabooks.com/glossary.

File Formats

QBO
QuickBooks Online import format. A proprietary Intuit file format containing bank transactions that imports directly into QuickBooks bank feeds without column mapping.
QIF
Quicken Interchange Format. An older Intuit format still supported by QuickBooks Desktop. Less common than QBO for current QuickBooks Online users.
OFX
Open Financial Exchange. An open standard for financial data exchange supported by QuickBooks, Xero, Sage, and most major accounting platforms.
IIF
Intuit Interchange Format. Used for importing list data (chart of accounts, customer lists) and transactions into QuickBooks Desktop.
CSV
Comma-Separated Values. A plain text format where each row is a transaction and columns are separated by commas. Universally supported but requires column mapping when importing into accounting software.

Technology Terms

OCR (Optical Character Recognition)
Technology that converts scanned document images into machine-readable text. Required for processing scanned PDF bank statements that lack an embedded text layer.
MICR (Magnetic Ink Character Recognition)
The encoded numbers printed at the bottom of checks in a specialized font. Contains routing number, account number, and check number. AI extraction tools can read MICR from check images.
AI Categorization
Automated assignment of accounting categories to transactions based on payee name, description, and historical patterns. Zera Books categorizes against QuickBooks/Xero chart of accounts with 95%+ accuracy.

Accounting Terms

Bank Reconciliation
The process of matching transactions in accounting records against bank statement entries, resolving discrepancies, and confirming balances agree after accounting for timing differences.
Chart of Accounts
The structured list of all accounts in a company's general ledger — assets, liabilities, equity, revenue, and expense accounts — used to categorize all financial transactions.
Multi-Account Statement
A bank statement PDF that contains transactions from multiple accounts (e.g., checking and savings on the same document). Requires account-level splitting before import into accounting software.
Duplicate Transaction
A transaction that appears twice in accounting records, typically from importing overlapping date ranges. AI tools like Zera Books detect and flag duplicates before they reach the ledger.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a QBO file?

A QBO file is a proprietary QuickBooks format for importing bank transactions. It contains date, amount, payee, and memo fields formatted for direct import into QuickBooks bank feeds — no column mapping required.

What does OCR mean in accounting software?

OCR (Optical Character Recognition) converts scanned document images into machine-readable text. In accounting, it enables extraction of transaction data from scanned PDF statements that don't have an embedded text layer.

What is bank reconciliation?

Bank reconciliation is matching a company's internal accounting records against bank statement entries, confirming balances agree after accounting for outstanding checks and deposits in transit.
