Why General PDF Converters Fail for Bank Statements

A general PDF-to-Excel conversion exports whatever the parser reads from the PDF's text layer. For bank statements, this creates three common problems:

  1. Column misalignment: Debit and credit columns on either side of a running balance column often merge into a single column
  2. Date formatting: Dates extracted as raw strings don't sort or filter correctly in Excel
  3. Multi-account mixing: Statements containing checking and savings accounts on the same pages get combined into one output without separation

What "Clean" Excel Output Looks Like

A well-structured bank statement Excel output has: one transaction per row, consistent date format (YYYY-MM-DD or MM/DD/YYYY), separate debit and credit columns (not a single +/- amount), normalized description text, and account identifier in a separate column for multi-account statements.
